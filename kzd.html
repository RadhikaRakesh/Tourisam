Tourist spots in Kozhikode

Pazhassi Raja Archaeological Museum
Pazhassi Raja Archaeological Museum is a museum and art gallery in Kozhikode, Kerala. The museum has a rich collection of historical artifacts from 1000 BC to 200 AD.

Thusharagiri Waterfalls
Thusharagiri waterfall is located in the Western Ghats of Kozhikode district. The place is crowded with numerous visitors throughout the year as the fall cascades down as three magnificent waterfalls. Thusharagiri waterfall is located in a small tribal village, sharing the name of the fall. The place is also hub of trekkers and rock climbers. You can take an interesting trek towards Lakkidi Ghat from here.

Manachira
Mananchira Square is a park in the centre of Kozhikode, Kerala state, southern India. The historic maithan, adjacent to Mananchira has been converted into an acadia with trees and plants, an artificial hill, shrubs, sculpture, an open-air theatre, and a musical fountain.

Beypore
Beypore or Beypur is an ancient port town and a locality town in Kozhikode district in the state of Kerala, India. The place was formerly known as Vaypura / Vadaparappanad. Tippu Sultan, ruler of Mysore, named the town "Sultan Pattanam".
